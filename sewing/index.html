Overview

The Neill-Wycik Sewing Room is a work and design space that allows members fo the co-op to design and create their own projects that involve the use of sewing machines, sergers, ironing boards and a large workspace. Due to the complexity of the machines in the Sewing Room, our ground rules and proper orientation are necessary for the safe and proper use of all equipment. Proper training will minimize the risk of injury and property damage. This is a professional workspace. Most who use this space use it for work or school purposes. Please be respectful and adhere to the rules of the space. Report damaged or dirty equipment, and please don’t mess with the machines.
We support a cooperative culture where:

  • Everyone has responsibility for learning the right way of using devices
  • Everyone shares responsibility for safety
  • We support one another and are receptive to feedback.
  • Innovation is encouraged
  • Positive experiences are created

This is a space to have FUN, LEARN, be PRODUCTIVE, and work SAFELY. Please adhere to posted safety and equipment guidelines, report damaged or unclean equipment, and ask questions.

Safety Guidelines


Report all Injuries/Incidents

  • Immediately report ALL accidents, injuries, or incidents to security at front desk.
  • Immediately discontinue use of the tool(s) or equipment if it becomes unsafe, damaged, or is not working properly.
  • Notify a Sewing Room Manager or Assistant Manager.

Safe Conduct Agreement

  • Use the space and equipment safely and leave the space and equipment in clean and working condition.
  • Do not use a device if you are not sure how to use it.
  • Operation of the Serger is restricted. Please note the manual.
  • Abide by SAC, safety, and fire regulations and comply with the rules and regulations of the equipment, whether posted or otherwise imposed by SAC.

Prepare

  • Safety is your top priority when using the Sewing Room. If you are not sure what to do, ask.
  • Be aware of locations of first aid, fire, and safety equipment
  • Never use a tool unless you’ve been trained to use it safely
  • Do not work when tired or in a hurry
  • Do not cause a distraction, fool around, or startle anyone while either one of you is using a tool

Use Protective Gear & Dress Right

  • Do not wear loose-fitting clothing around moving or rotating machinery.
  • Remove ties, jewelry, lanyards, etc., especially around moving or rotating machinery.
  • Tie back long hair.
  • Wear suitable gloves when handling hot objects or sharp-edged items.

Use Tools Correctly

  • Use tools how they are designed to be used.
  • Never use a broken tool. Report any broken tools or machinery to a Sewing Room manager immediately.
  • Do not remove tools from the Sewing Room.
  • Never walk away from a machine, Iron or device that is still on.
  • Never tamper with any saftey safety features on any oof the equipment. Operate machinery according to recommended procedures and with safety guards in place, as applicable.

Food and Drinks

  • Drink and food are NOT permitted.

First Aid Equipments

  • There is a first aid equipment kit near the door.


Training


Before any member can utilize the space, there will be a requirement for them to undergo an orientation session with either the manager of the space or either of the assistant managers. This training will include, but not be limited to:

  • The locations and proper usage of the Emergency Supplies (First Aid Kits and Eyewash Station) inside of the space
  • An overview of the procedural rules of the space (including occupancy limits for the space, cleaning requirements of the space, sign in procedures, guest policy, etc ... )
  • Training on the usage of the various machines available inside of the space, particularly the sergerand industrial sewing machine.)

Ground Rules for the Sewing Room


The ground rules for the Sewing Room shall be as follows. Violation of any of the rules of the space will lead to loss of usage privileges:

  • You need an orientation in order to use the Sewing Room.
  • You need to sign in and out of the Sewing Room at The Security Desk.
  • If you are about to leave and someone wants to use the faciility , you must both go to the front desk to transfer responsibility of the facility.
  • Leave the room in a clean and orderly state.
  • Please keep the door shut to the facility at all times.
  • It is your responsibility to check and ensure that the equipment is in safe operating order before and after using the equipment.
  • Report any damage discovered to Security immediately.
  • While you have signed out the facility; you assume full responsibility for any damage to the equipment. The exception being a failure due to manufacturers defect.
  • Nothing is to be removed or added to the facility or equipment without approval of the Facility Manager.
  • Please not to tamper with the equipment.
  • Only members who have received an orientation to the Sewing Room Facility and all of the equipment of the facility may sign out the keys to the facility.
  • Only "Schmetz" brand needles may be used in our serger.
  • Report any damage discovered to Security immediately.
  • Liquids or food are NOT permitted in the Sewing Room at anytime.
  • Please clip threads close to the spool.
  • Storage of personal projects See Project Storage below.

Project Storage


You are allowed to store personal projects in the sewing room, it must be clearly labelled with your name and contact info of some kind. Keep all storage to a minimum, Neill-Wycik is not responsible for lost or damaged items that you may keep in the sewing room. A yearly purge does occur in May, items not claimed by then are either donated or added to the shared supplies cabinet so please keep this in mind when storing items in this facility.

Sewing Machines and Rules



Our Sewing Machines are available for use by members who have recieved an orientation to the Sewing Room, and have been trained on their use. Prior to using any of the equipment in the Sewing Room, you must complete the mandatory Orientation Quiz, A Code of Conduct and Safety Quiz. The following rules must be followed with respect to using any of the Sewing Machines:

  • Do not wear loose-fitting clothing around moving or rotating machinery.
  • Remove ties, jewelry, lanyards, etc., especially around moving or rotating machinery.
  • Tie back long hair.
  • For reasons of safety, ensure that you are only using "Schmetz" brand needles in our serger.

Our Serger

Our Juki™ MO 3616 is an industrial serger / overlocking sewing machine that makes: 3 thread, 4 thread, and 5 thread stiches. Please see the video here for a more practical review of the machine's use.

Our Single Lockstich Sewing Machine

Our Juki DDL8100e sewing machine is a single needle overstitch machine used primarily for alterations. It is designed for sewing light to medium weight materials such as cotton, polyester, knits, denim and fabrics. The machine sews up to 5500 stitches per minute and is suitable for stitching such products as blouses, shirts, men's, ladies and children's apparel, uniforms, overalls, lingerie, etc. Please watch the Getting Started video for a better understanding of the machines uses and how to thread the machine.

Our 4 Stitch Industrial Serger

Our Juki™ MO-6000S is used for run stitching in general fabrics to make such things as Under shirts, Polo shirts, T-Shirts, Jumpers, Skirts, and Sportswear. There are two important types of stitching. One is called a backstitch because the needle goes into the fabric behind the thread of the previous stitch. By contrast, a forward stitch, usually called a running stitch, is made by inserting the needle into the fabric in front of the thread from the previous stitch. See the video for how a running stitch can be applied.

Our Singer™ Stylist 776 Sewing Machine

Our Singer™ Stylist 776 is an industrial sewing machine used for a variety of stitching. The machine for can sewing button holes, do patter stitching, Zig Zag Stitching, Straight Stitching with a Twin Needle, Temporary Stitching, and Speed Blasting.
