Overview

The Neill-Wycik Steamer is a space that allow members of the co-op to host events sponsored by SAC or that they themselves sponsor and use the A/V System or for personal use, or for use with a SAC sponsored event. Due to the nature of the space and components inside the space, both in terms of cost and safety, it is necessary to lay out the ground rules for usage of the space to ensure. proper training of students who wish to use the space and minimize the risk of injuries and property damage as a result.
We support a cooperatve culture where:

  • Everyone has responsibility for learning the right way of using devices
  • Everyone shares responsibility for safety
  • We support one another and are receptive to feedback.
  • Cooperation is encouraged
  • Positive experiences are created

This is a space to have FUN, LEARN, be CREATIVE, and play SAFELY. Please adhere to posted safety and equipment guidelines, report damaged or unclean equipment, and ask questions.

Safety Guidelines


Report all Injuries/Incidents

  • Immediately report ALL accidents, injuries, or incidents to security at front desk.
  • Immediately discontinue use of the equipment if it becomes unsafe, damaged, or is not working properly.
  • Notify a Steamer Manager or Assistant Manager.

Safe Conduct Agreement

  • Use the space and equipment safely and leave the space and equipment in clean and working condition.
  • Do not use a device if you are not sure how to use it.
  • Operation of the 3D Projector is restricted. Please note the manual.
  • Abide by SAC, safety, and fire regulations and comply with the rules and regulations of the equipment, whether posted or otherwise imposed by SAC.

Prepare

  • Safety is your top priority when using the Steamer. If you are not sure what to do, ask.
  • Be aware of locations of first aid, and safety equipment.
  • Never use any equipment unless you’ve been trained to use it safely.
  • Use a step stool to reach extension cords or items which are out of reach. Do not climb on tables or chairs to reach for something.
  • Do not cause a distraction, fool around, or startle anyone while either one of you is using a tool.

Use Equipment Correctly

  • Use all equipment how they are designed to be used.
  • Never attempt to use broken or faulty equipment or components. Report any broken equipment or components to a Steamer Facility Manager or Aiisitant immediately.
  • Do not remove tools or equipment from the Steamer.
  • Never walk away from any equipment that is still on.
  • Never tamper with any equipment settings or features. Operate the Audio System according to recommended procedures and with the cabinet doors open.

Food and Drinks

  • Glass containers are NOT permitted.
  • Drink and food are permitted. Also, you are responsible for cleaning up after yorself and not leaving the space in an untidy state.
  • Alcohol is permitted in this space.

First Aid Equipment

  • There is a first aid equipment kit located at the Main Entrance to the Steamer and also at the Bar Area.


Training


Before any member can utilize the space, there will be a requirement for them to undergo an orientation session with either the manager of the space or either of the assistant managers. This training will include, but not be limited to:

  • The locations and proper usage of the Emergency Supplies (First Aid Kits and Eyewash Station) inside of the space.
  • An overview of the procedural rules of the space (including occupancy limits for the space, cleaning requirements of the space, sign in procedures, guest policy, etc ... )
  • Training on the usage of the various equipment available inside of the space, particularly the 3D Projector, Light Controller and Audio/Video System, and:
  • A practical test of said training in the form of a basic walkthrough of how the eqipment operates (preceeded by the completion of the online quiz linked above on this site).

Ground Rules for the Steamer


The ground rules for the Steamer shall be as follows. Violation of any of the rules of the space will lead to loss of usage privileges:

  • The occupancy limit of the Steamer at any given time shall be llmited to 76 persons.
  • No untrained members or guests are allowed to operate any equipment in the Steamer under any circumstances.
  • No glass is allowed in the Steamer under any circumstances.
  • All users of the space must both sign into the space when they enter and sign out of the space when they leave.
  • All users must sign out the key set from security.
  • All users must book the steamer to reserve useage.
  • All users wishing to cancel a booking must do so 60 mins prior to their reservation at the Security Desk. Failure will result in the loss of privilidge to the Steamer for one week.
  • All users must remove the Garbage and Recycling to the respective Recycling Area in the Garage and garbage to the Garbage Area in the Garage.
  • All users must leave the room in a clean state. Users are responsible for bleaning up after themselves. Please practise common courtesy towards your fellow members.
  • All users must ensure the audio and video equiptment is powered down before they discontinue use of the space.
  • Any and all damages or emergencies must immediately be reported to both security and the Facility Manager/Assistants.

Personal Storage


Storage of any personal items in the Steamer is not permtted. SAC does not in any conceivable way wish to take responsibility for the safe storage of your personal items.

3D Projector Rules



3D projector is available for watching movies, on our Bluray player or with an application like Youtube or Netflix. You may also hook up your own laptop and use the projector for working on presentations for class. The following rules must be adhered to for the benefit of all member users :

  • All 3D projector settings, calibration and alignment of the projector is never to be altered by members prior to or during use.
  • The 3D projector is never to be left on after member use and it is the responsibility of the member user to ensure the projector is properly powered down after each use. Bulbs for the projector are expensive and premature replacement due to misuse is deemed by SAC as an unnecessary expense.
  • Any instance of instance of a fault with the 3D projector must immediately be reported to both security and facility manager.
  • Steamer 3D projector may be used only for lawful purposes and Neill-Wycik Steamer reserves the right to refuse any member who uses the projector to display anything pronographic, lewd, or unlawful from continued use of the Steamer Facility. The 3D projector cannot be used to display content material that is:
    • Prohibited by provincial or federal law.
    • In violation of the intellectual property rights of others. For example, reproducing material that is subject to copyright, patent, or trademark protection.
    • Lewd, malicious, or otherwise unsuitable for creating a welcoming and inclusive public space.

What is important to know about our 3D projector?

It offers a choice of Normal, Full, Wide Zoom, Zoom, and Anamorphic Zoom. The other setting on the Screen menu is Overscan. The Sony offers only Overscan off and on options, and no masking alternative (as it maintains 1:1 pixel mapping).

  • The projector presents the best quality image when this setting is left on normal.

    • Where do I start?

    If you are new to using our 3D projector and wish to learn the basics, start with this youtube video. Promotional Video It has easy ot use, but requires a warm up time for the lamp of 5 minutes from power on. How do I use the 3D projector? You will be required to complete a one-time mandatory 3D Projector orientation and sign a Steamer User Agreement. Once you have passed the training with 100% and completed the User Agreement you can use thre 3D projector if any problems arise you can contact the Steamer Manager or Assistant Manager (Email).

    What can I project?

    While the possibilities may seem endless, there are some limitations with the projector. Since it is not a 4K projector it cannot project 4K content. While you can display video from your computer desktop, you will have to be familiar with how to resize your desktop image so icons are clearly visible. The Steamer equipment may be used only for lawful purposes. Equipment cannot be used to display or project previously-mentioned content.

    How big is the projected image?

    While it is dependant on the distance back from the screen and how big the screen is, the current set up will project an image size of 110 inches wide by 69.4 inches high (279.4 cm high by 175.5 cm high).

    How many people can comfortably view the projected image?

    It depends first on the occupancy capacity of the Steamer which is 75 people, however a range of between 9.5 feet (3.3m) closest to 20 feet (6.09m) farthest gives an ideal range.We can easily accomodate between 15 and 40 people and provide a comfortable viewing experience. Things to remember:

    • Popcorn or other snacks.
    • Seating (red vinyl chairs and beige theatre chairs are for your use in the Steamer).
    • Put the screen up when you are done.
    • Power off the 3D projector when you are done.
    • Clean up your garbage and recycling when you are done watching your movie.
    • Stack the red chairs up against the wall when you are done.
    • If you have less than 10 people in the Steamer you can have alcohol.
    • If you have 10 or more people present Security must be paid to attend your event for you to have alcohol.

    Video Screen Rules

    Video Screen is available for watching movies, on our Bluray player or with an application like Youtube or Netflix. You may also hook up your own laptop and use the projector for working on presentations for class. The following rules must be adhered to for the benefit of all member users :

    • Always retract ( roll up ) the video screen when you are done using the A/V system.
    • Never attempt tp clean the Video Screen on your own under any circumstances.
    • Any instance of instance of a fault with the Video Screen must immediately be reported to both security and facility manager.
    • Steamer Video Screen may be used only for lawful purposes and Neill-Wycik Steamer reserves the right to refuse any member who uses the Video Screen to display anything pronographic, lewd, or unlawful from continued use of the Steamer Facility. The Video Screen cannot be used to display content material that is:
      • Prohibited by provincial or federal law.
      • In violation of the intellectual property rights of others. For example, reproducing material that is subject to copyright, patent, or trademark protection.
      • Lewd, malicious, or otherwise unsuitable for creating a welcoming and inclusive public space.

    What is important to know about our Video Screen?

    It is a motorized screen which can be lowered by a switch inside the DJ Booth. The screen is triggered by 120 Volts of electricity by toggeling a the light switch inside the lower cabinet of the DJ Booth.

  • Our screen is manually trigged and does not drop when a video source it turned on
  • Our screen can be cleaned with simple cleaning materials
  • Our screen has good off angel viewing
  • Our size is ideal for HDTV, and all other 16:9 video content
  • You have to manually retract the screen when you are done using it before leaving the Steamer
  • The projector has been calibrated to completely fill the screen under almost every viewing situation
  • Our screen must never be cleaned with harsh chemicals or highly concentrated detergent.

    Mixing Panel Allen and Heath XONE42


    What is important to know about our audio mixing panel?

    It is the connection point for all audio signals for the Audio Video System. Here are some important features of the Mixer:

    • Every input (slider) is clearly labeled to indicate what sound it controls
    • 4 dual stereo channels (2 phono/line, 2 line/line)
    • 3 band EQ +6dB to total kill
    • Rotary X-FX control for routing to external FX
    • VCF filter with resonance & frequency controls
    • VCA Crossfader with curve control
    • Mix ouputs on balanced XLR
    • 9 segment channel meters
    • Illuminated lightpipes showing status of performance controls
    • USB port with one stereo up to PC and one stereo back
    • Independent booth output
    • XLR microphone input with 2-band EQ and level control
    • 1 headphone output (1/4”)
  • The Audio Mixer must never be left on when you are done and ready to leave the Steamer. Always remember to turn the power on to the mixer first before the amplifier to avoid a loud burst of sound from the speakers.

    • Standby/Go facility with remote control capability
