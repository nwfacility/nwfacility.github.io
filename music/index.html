Overview

The Neill-Wycik Music Room is a rehersal and practise space that allow members of the co-op to unwind, create and play their own music. The guitars are all donations from past members, while the Piano and Drum Kit were purchased by SAC for Neill-Wycik members' use and enjoyment. Due to the nature of the instruments inside the space both in terms of cost and safety, it is necessary to lay out the ground rules for useage of the space to ensure proper training of the members who wish to use the space and minimize the risk of injuries and property damage as a result. We support creativity, the enjoyment of music and a culture where:

  • Everyone has responsibility for learning the right way of using instruments
  • Everyone shares responsibility for safety
  • We support one another and are receptive to feedback
  • Innovation is encouraged
  • Positive experiences are created

This is a space to have FUN, LEARN, be PRODUCTIVE, and play SAFELY. Please adhere to posted safety and equipment guidelines, report damaged or unclean equipment, and ask questions.

Safety Guidelines


Report all Injuries/Incidents

  • Immediately report ALL accidents, injuries, or incidents to security at front desk.
  • Immediately discontinue use of the equipment if it becomes unsafe, damaged, or is not working properly.
  • Notify a Music Room Manager or Assistant Manager.

Safe Conduct Agreement

  • Use the space and instruments safely and leave the space and equipment in clean and working condition.
  • Do not use an instrument if you are not sure how to use it.
  • Abide by SAC, safety, and fire regulations and comply with the rules and regulations of the equipment, whether posted or otherwise imposed by SAC.

Stay Safe

  • Safety is your top priority when using the Music Room. If you are not sure what to do, ask.
  • Be aware of locations of first aid, and hand sanitizer.
  • Never use an instrument unless you’ve been trained to use it properly.
  • Use a step stool to reach extension cords or tools which are out of reach. Do not climb on tables or chairs to reach for something.
  • Do not plug in more than one amplifier to the Piano A/C Outlet as this will trip the breaker and leave you in the dark.
  • Do not cause a distraction, fool around, or startle anyone while either one of you is using an instrument.

Food and Drinks

  • Food and liquids are ABSOLUTELY forbidden in the Music Room. Breaking this rule can result in you losing access to the facility. Also, you are responsible for cleaning up after yourself and not leaving the space in an untidy state.
  • Alcohol is not permitted in this space.

Use Instruments Correctly

  • Use instruments how they are designed to be used.
  • Never use a broken instrument. Report any instruments, furniture, or fixtures to a Music Room Manager immediately (take photos and attach them in your email).
  • Do not remove instruments, furniture, or fixtures from the Music Room.
  • Never leave electrical cables, interconnect cables, or microphone cables strewn about the space as it causes a trip hazzard.

First Aid Equipment

  • There is a first aid equipment kit located at the Main Entrance to the Music Room and at the Front Security Desk.

Preparing to Use the Drum Kit

If you follow these simle steps, you should be able to turn on, play, and even record your sessions:

  • Plug the drum kit control module, located on the left of the kit, into a standard power outlet. All drum modules should already be connected as they need to be.
  • Turn the drum kit on using the power button, which is located on the top right corner of the control module.
  • The headphone jack is on the front edge of the control module, near the left corner. Please note that this is a 1/4" (6.35mm) socket, so most headphones will require the use of a 3.5mm-to-1/4" adapter. The Music Room has one that can be used.
  • The outputs for recording are on the back of the control module, next to the drum module inputs, at the left of the rear. These can be plugged into the M-Track audio interface using 1/4" instrument cables. Recording can be done in mono, using just the left output (either channel of the M-Track can be used for the input), or in stereo, using both outputs and both M-Track inputs. For a stereo recording setup, connect the Left output to input 1 on the M-Track, and the Right output to input 2 on the M-Track.
  • The wheel on the front of the control module can be used to select the set of drum sounds you wish to use. The output volume can be adjusted with the volume buttons, also on the front of the control module.

Guitars and Amplifiers Important Facts


Guitar strings will inevitably stretch and occasionally break due to prolonged usage. This is normal and strings will be replaced by SAC as required. Please report any problems with the guitar strings to the facility manager in order for replacements to be secured. However, any breakage discovered to be the result of rough or improper handling of the instruments will result in the cost of replacements being borne by the member concerned.
