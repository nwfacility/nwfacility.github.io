Overview

The Neill-Wycik Games Room is a space that allow members of the co-op to unwind and chill out with other members. Getting a few people together for a Pong-Pong Turnament, or a Pool Turnament is a great way to unwind and blow off a little steam. Due to the nature of the components inside the space, both in terms of cost and safety, it is necessary to lay out the ground rules for usage of the space to ensure proper training of students who wish to use the space and minimize the risk of injuries and property damage as a result.
We support a cooperative culture where:

  • Everyone has responsibility for learning the right way of using equipment.
  • Everyone shares responsibility for safety.
  • We support one another and are receptive to feedback.
  • Feedback is encouraged.
  • Positive experiences are created.

This is a space to have FUN, UNWIND, be COOPERATIVE, and PLAY SAFELY. Please adhere to posted safety and equipment guidelines, report damaged or unclean equipment, and ask questions.

Safety Guidelines


Report all Injuries/Incidents

  • Immediately report ALL accidents, injuries, or incidents to security at front desk.
  • Immediately discontinue use of the equipment if it becomes unsafe, damaged, or is not working properly.
  • Notify a Games Room Manager or Assistant Manager.

Safe Conduct Agreement

  • Use the space and Games Tables and Darts safely.
  • Leave the space and equipment in clean and working condition.
  • Do not use a device if you are not sure how to use it.
  • Abide by SAC, safety, and fire regulations and comply with the rules and regulations of the equipment, whether posted or otherwise imposed by SAC.

Use Games Tables and Darts Correctly

  • Use equipment how they are designed to be used.
  • Never use any broken item. Report any damaged or broken Games Tables, Darts, furniture, or fixtures to a Music Room Manager immediately (take photos and attach them in your email).
  • Do not remove equipment from the Games Room.

Food and Drinks

  • Drink and food are permitted. Also, you are responsible for cleaning up after yorself and not leaving the space in an untidy state.
  • Alcohol is not permitted in this space.

First Aid Equipment

  • There is a first aid equipment kit on the wall of the room.


Training


Before any member can utilize the space, there will be a requirement for them to undergo an orientation session with either the manager of the space or either of the assistant managers. This training will include, but not be limited to:

  • The locations and proper usage of the Emergency Supplies (First Aid Kits and Eyewash Station) inside of the space
  • An overview of the procedural rules of the space (including occupancy limits for the space, cleaning requirements of the space, sign in procedures, guest policy, etc ... )
  • Orientation training on the usage and care of of the various instruments available inside of the space, particularly the Pool Table and Ping Pong Table, and:Training on the usage of the various tools available inside of the space, particularly the chesstable irons and 3D printer, and:
  • A practical test of said training in the form of an online quiz (proper set up and useage of the Ping Pong Table and when to report issues)

Ground Rules for the Games Room


The ground rules for the Games Room shall be as follows. Violation of any of the rules of the space will lead to loss of usage privileges:

  • You Need to Sign into the Games Room. How? You need to exchange your Member ID card at Security to get the key.
  • No Glass Containers in the Games Room. If you bring in glass you will be asked to remove the glass in order to use the facility.
  • No Excessively Loud Music. Any audio device including the Games Room Radio, cannot be played so loud as to interfere with the members living in nearby units. Be respectful and courteous to others. If asked to turn down the music then do so.
  • Please report any damage. Report any damage, or facility abuse / misuse to Security, and the Games Room Facility Manager.
  • Please put all Garbage / Recycling in the bins provided. Please remove all Recycling & Garbage to the Garage Bins. Food Waste, & Recycling left behind only attracts Wildlife!
  • Leave the room in a clean state.
  • You are responsible for cleaning up after yourself. Please practice common courtesy towards your fellow members!
  • Any and all damage or emergencies must immediately be reported to both Security and the Facility Manager/Assistants.

    Foosball Rules Explained

    The 6 basic Foosball Rules

    • Flip A Coin - A coin flip decides who serves the foosball to start the foosball game. After the first goal is scored, the rule is that the team who was last scored on gets to serve the next ball. This rule keeps the game fair for both opponents. The foosball must be touched by a man before the ball goes into a goal for a legal goal to occur.
    • No Spinning - A spin is a rule for when the rod completes a 360 degree rotation without touching the ball. This is deemed an illegal move in the rules of foosball.
    • No Jarring - Jarring is a rule for when a player slams his or her rods against the wall of the foosball table in an attempt to distract or jar the foosball loose from the other player's possession. The rule is that there is no jarring allowed at any point during a game. This is not the same as when a player on the opposite team moves his or her foosball rods back and forth in an attempt to play defense. The key this rule is when a player hits the wall of the table with enough force to jar the ball or distract the opponent. That is why this is a subjective rule that may be called more strict by certain foosball player
    • Dead Balls - If a foosball stops in the middle of two opposing team rods, the ball must be picked up and reserved. The serve goes back to the team that was last scored on. If a dead ball occurs on the defensive side of the table behind the defensive 2 bar, the defensive player must move the ball back into playing position. This rule is in place so players don't cause a dead ball on purpose in order to force a reserve.
    • Out of Play - If a ball comes off the table area or hits a top rail and comes back into the table area, the ball is ruled out of play and must be reserved by the team that was last scored on.
    • 5 Bar Passing - These 5 bar passing rules are a little complex if you have never played tournament foosball. These passing rules do not apply to the other foosball rods, just the 5 bar. You are required to pass the ball within a 10 second time period from your 5 bar rod to the offensive 3 bar rod directly after a serve. After the initial serve, you then are allowed 15 seconds to pass from the 5 bar. A ball cannot be completely stopped on the 5 bar and passed forward to the 3 bar, it must be moving and touch 2 men on the 5 bar before being advanced. Additionally, the ball can only touch the wall a total of 2 times in a row (before touching a man again on the 5 bar) before being advanced. However, it can touch the ball a 3rd time if it is part of the pass.
